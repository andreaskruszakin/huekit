Color search

1 · Dot ring

The trigger icon blown up: a radial ring of 12 curated hue dots. Hover enlarges, one click selects and closes. The baseline — zero learning curve, fastest possible path from intent to token. Tradeoff: no shade control; 12 hues is all you get.

2 · Hue ring + shades

Two-stage radial: the outer ring picks a hue, an inner arc blooms with five tints and shades of it. Trades one extra click for shade precision without ever showing a saturation square. Wins when "light red vs. dark red" actually matters to search results.

3 · Orbital scrub

Continuous: drag around the wheel like a dial — a needle sweeps the hue circle, nearby dots magnify toward the pointer, the center previews the color live. Release to commit; arrow keys step the hue. Trades click precision for a tactile, continuous feel. Wins when browsing color is part of the pleasure.

4 · Dot grid

No wheel: a flat curated palette grid — vivid hues, muted tones, neutrals — with a live preview row on top. Trades the radial motif for scannability and a palette that can grow without crowding a circle. Wins when the palette is editorial and finite, not a color space.

5 · Inline bloom

No popover: clicking the trigger blooms the dots horizontally into the search bar itself; picking one collapses the row into a color token sitting in the input. Trades UI surface (no room for shades or labels) for never leaving the search context. Wins when color is a quick filter, not a decision.
